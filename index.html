— The conversation that earns the prescription
0–3min
Welcome & frame

Confirm time, set expectations, get permission to record. Acknowledge: "I read your notes — I'm not going to make you start from scratch."

Sarah's story · what's worked, what hasn't

Open question. Don't interrupt for the first 4 minutes. Listen for: what's been tried, who's invalidated her, what her partner thinks, what the breaking point was.

Share screen. Walk Sarah through the developmental pyramid — bottom up. Annotate Liam's specific position on each layer. Run the 6 reflex screens together. End on the reframe.

— Internal · the refund mechanic The completion refund is doing three jobs at once. ① It makes the sticker price feel smaller — every tier shows a slashed-then-effective treatment, anchored to the under-$10K psychological line for Foundation. ② It drives compliance — families do the protocol because they earn the money back, which means they actually get results. ③ Most families never take the cash — they apply the earned refund as a credit toward Continuing Care, which becomes the bridge to year-two LTV. Tiered refunds: Foundation $1,500 · Accelerated $2,000 · Immersive $2,500. The framing on the call is a thank-you for trust, not a discount or a closing tactic — it has to feel like a gift, not a hook. Pricing anchored to Pricing Model v1.4.3; the refund mechanic ships as a Continuing Care credit earned at Wk 12 completion + CC enrollment.
— Internal · the proof slide (02/08) The case studies on slide 02/08 are illustrative composites for the strategy demo — they reflect realistic-feeling patterns from the 41-family cohort, but every case shown needs to be sourced from a real chart, get explicit parent written consent, and pass MD signoff before it ships in customer-facing material. The cohort headline numbers ("8 of 11 Sensory-Motor Pattern cases reached age-typical speech volume by Wk 12") need to be reconciled against the actual cohort data and refreshed quarterly. Pulling this together is in the to-build column flagged in the Closing slide. Until those steps are complete, this slide is a placeholder for what real proof looks like — not the deployed version.
